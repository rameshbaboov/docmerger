Business Letter Templates: New Service Introduction

Below are five professional letter templates for introducing new services of an existing company. Each template is approximately 100 words and includes placeholders for personalized information.

Letter 1: New Service Announcement to Existing Clients

Word Count: ~100 words | Tone: Professional & Confident 
Subject: Exciting News: Introducing [New Service Name] for [Client's Company Name]

Dear [Client Name],

Thank you for your continued partnership with [Your Company Name]. We are constantly striving to enhance our offerings to better serve your evolving needs, which is why we are thrilled to introduce our new service: [New Service Name].

This service is designed to help you [Achieve Key Benefit, e.g., streamline your workflow and reduce operational costs] through [Briefly describe service, e.g., our advanced, cloud-based project management platform].

As a valued client, we would like to offer you an exclusive introductory demonstration. Please let us know a time that works for you next week.

Best regards,

[Your Name]
[Your Job Title]
[Your Company Name]
[Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This works because it acknowledges the existing relationship first, making the client feel valued. It directly connects the new service to a client benefit and offers a personalized, low-pressure next step.
Tips for Adaptation: For a more formal tone, use "We are pleased to announce..." For a more casual tone, use "We're excited to tell you about...". To target a specific client segment, mention how the service solves a challenge relevant to their industry.

Letter 2: Cross-Selling New Service to a Segment of Clients

Word Count: ~100 words | Tone: Professional & Insightful 
Subject: A New Solution to [Address Specific Pain Point] for [Client's Company Name]

Dear [Client Name],

Based on your company's success in [Client's Industry/Specific Area], we believe you will be particularly interested in our newest offering: [New Service Name].

We designed this service to help businesses like yours overcome [Specific Challenge, e.g., scaling customer support without increasing overhead] by providing [Service Description, e.g., an on-demand, outsourced support team trained in your specific protocols].

This service integrates seamlessly with the [Existing Product/Service] you currently use. We have seen it drive impressive results in [Metric, e.g., improving customer satisfaction scores by 25%].

Would a 15-minute call on [Date] be useful to explore its potential for you?

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
[Your Job Title]
[Your Company Name]
[Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This is effective because it demonstrates that you understand the client's business and their potential needs. It personalizes the offer and provides social proof with a specific, results-oriented metric.
Tips for Adaptation: For a more engaging tone, phrase the challenge as a question: "Are you finding it difficult to scale your customer support?" For a complex version, include a brief one-sentence case study.

Letter 3: Internal Announcement to Employees

Word Count: ~100 words | Tone: Engaging & Informative 
Subject: Announcing Our New [New Service Name] Service!

Hi Team,

I'm excited to announce that we are officially launching a new service: [New Service Name]! This offering allows us to [Strategic Goal, e.g., enter the sustainable consulting market and provide more holistic solutions to our clients].

This launch is a testament to your hard work and feedback. The new service will involve collaboration across [Department 1] and [Department 2].

Please join us for a mandatory launch meeting on [Date] at [Time] in [Location/Video Link] where we will detail the service, its value proposition, and what this means for your roles.

Let's make this launch a success!

Best,

[Your Name]
[Your Job Title]
Commentary: This works internally because it generates excitement, acknowledges the team's contribution, and provides clear, immediate next steps. It fosters a sense of shared purpose and prepares employees for the changes ahead.
Tips for Adaptation: For a larger company, use a more formal tone with "Dear Employees" and "Sincerely." For a more engaging tone in a startup, include emojis and more enthusiastic language.

Letter 4: Announcement to Past Clients / Inactive Subscribers

Word Count: ~100 words | Tone: Warm & Re-engaging 
Subject: We've added something new for you, [Client Name]!

Dear [Client Name],

We hope this message finds you well. It's been a while since we last connected, and we've been hard at work expanding our capabilities at [Your Company Name].

We are writing today to introduce [New Service Name], which helps businesses [Achieve Key Benefit, e.g., leverage data analytics for smarter decision-making]. Given your past focus on [Past Client's Goal/Project], we thought this might be highly relevant to you.

Many of our clients are using this service to [Quantifiable Result, e.g., uncover new growth opportunities and increase revenue by an average of 15%].

Would you be interested in a brief update call to hear more?

Warmly,

[Your Name]
[Your Job Title]
[Your Company Name]
[Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This letter is effective for re-engagement because it is respectful of the lapsed time, provides a legitimate reason for reconnecting, and specifically ties the new service to the client's past interests or behaviors.
Tips for Adaptation: To sound more formal, remove "We hope this message finds you well" and start with "We are writing to inform you of a new service...". For a more casual tone, use "Hi [First Name]" and simpler language.

Letter 5: Strategic Partner & Vendor Announcement

Word Count: ~100 words | Tone: Collaborative & Strategic 
Subject: Enhancing Our Joint Value Proposition: Introducing [New Service Name]

Dear [Partner Contact Name],

As a valued partner of [Your Company Name], we want you to be among the first to know about our new service offering: [New Service Name].

This service provides [Target Market, e.g., mid-market manufacturing companies] with [Service Description, e.g., end-to-end supply chain visibility solutions]. We believe this creates a significant opportunity for us to collaborate on serving our mutual clients more comprehensively and driving additional value.

We are eager to explore how [New Service Name] can complement your offerings and strengthen our partnership. Would you be open to a brief meeting next week to discuss potential synergies?

Best regards,

[Your Name]
[Your Job Title]
[Your Company Name]
[Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This works for partners because it makes them feel included and important. It focuses on mutual benefit and collaboration rather than a one-sided announcement, framing the new service as an opportunity to enhance the relationship.
Tips for Adaptation: For a more formal tone, use "Dear [Title + Last Name]" and "We are pleased to inform you...". For a less formal partner relationship, use "Hi [First Name]" and a more conversational tone.
