Business Letter Templates

Requesting Referral from Existing Client (Medium Complexity)

Below are five professional letter templates for requesting a referral from an existing client. Each template is approximately 100 words and includes placeholders for personalized information, making it easy to adapt to your specific situation.

Letter 1: Direct Referral Request

Word Count: ~100 words | Tone: Professional & Direct | Complexity: Medium
Subject: Referral Request for [Prospective Client Company Name] Dear [Client Name], I hope this message finds you well. I'm writing to you today regarding [Prospective Client Company Name], a company that I believe could benefit from our services, much like [Client Company Name] has. Given our successful partnership and the positive results we've achieved together with [Specific Project or Outcome], I was wondering if you would be open to providing a brief referral letter or introduction. Your endorsement would be incredibly valuable as we begin conversations with them. I've attached a draft letter for your convenience, which you can modify as you see fit. Thank you for considering this request. Best regards, [Your Name] [Your Title] [Your Company Name] [Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This template is direct yet respectful, acknowledging the value of the client's endorsement. By mentioning specific positive outcomes, it reinforces why their referral would be meaningful.
Tips for Adaptation: For longer relationships, add a sentence recalling your history together. For more formal clients, use a more traditional letter structure with addresses.

Letter 2: Referral Request with Specific Ask

Word Count: ~105 words | Tone: Professional & Specific | Complexity: Medium
Subject: Would you be willing to provide a referral for [Your Company Name]? Dear [Client Name], I hope you're doing well. We're currently in discussions with [Prospective Client Company Name] and they've asked for references from our current clients. Given our productive collaboration on [Specific Project] and the [Specific Result] we achieved together, I thought you would be an ideal reference for our work. Would you be open to either: 1. Providing a brief written testimonial we can share, or 2. Allowing them to contact you directly for a short conversation? I completely understand if you're unable to assist at this time, but any support you can provide would be greatly appreciated. Sincerely, [Your Name] [Your Title] [Your Company Name] [Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This template provides clear options for the client, making it easy for them to choose how to help. The specific mention of the project and result makes the request more justified.
Tips for Adaptation: Adjust the specific options based on what you're most hoping to receive. For busy clients, emphasize the minimal time requirement.

Letter 3: Mutual Benefit Referral Request

Word Count: ~100 words | Tone: Collaborative & Professional | Complexity: Medium
Subject: Partnering for mutual growth with [Prospective Client Company Name] Dear [Client Name], I'm reaching out because we're exploring a partnership with [Prospective Client Company Name], an organization that I believe shares synergies with both our company and yours. As we begin conversations, your endorsement would carry significant weight. Our work together on [Specific Project] delivered [Specific Result], and I know they would value hearing about your experience. In return, I'd be happy to provide a reciprocal referral for your business should the opportunity arise. Would you be open to sharing a few sentences about your positive experience working with us? Thank you for your consideration. Warm regards, [Your Name] [Your Title] [Your Company Name] [Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This template frames the request as a mutually beneficial arrangement rather than a one-sided ask. The offer of reciprocity can make clients more willing to assist.
Tips for Adaptation: For clients in complementary industries, emphasize the potential synergy. For clients who might benefit from exposure, highlight how the referral might help them too.

Letter 4: Post-Project Success Referral Request

Word Count: ~95 words | Tone: Appreciative & Professional | Complexity: Medium
Subject: Following up on our successful project and a small request Dear [Client Name], Now that we've successfully completed [Recent Project Name], I wanted to thank you again for the opportunity to work together. The [Specific Positive Outcome] we achieved was particularly noteworthy. I'm writing today with a small request: we're speaking with [Prospective Client Company Name] about a similar project, and they've asked for client references. Given our positive experience together, would you be willing to provide a brief testimonial about your experience working with our team? Even a few sentences would be immensely helpful. Thank you for considering this request. Best regards, [Your Name] [Your Title] [Your Company Name] [Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This template leverages the timing of a recently completed successful project, when positive feelings are likely at their peak. The specific mention of the project outcome reinforces why their referral would be valuable.
Tips for Adaptation: Adjust based on the recency and success level of the project. For extremely successful projects, be more confident in the ask.

Letter 5: Warm Introduction Request

Word Count: ~100 words | Tone: Professional & Respectful | Complexity: Medium
Subject: Introduction request for [Prospective Client Company Name] Dear [Client Name], I hope you're having a productive week. I'm writing to you today regarding [Prospective Client Company Name], with whom we're hoping to establish a partnership. I understand you have a connection there through [Specific Connection - e.g., "your colleague John Smith", "the annual industry conference", etc.], and I was wondering if you would be comfortable making a warm introduction on our behalf. Alternatively, if an introduction isn't appropriate, would you consider providing a brief letter of reference that we could share with them? Thank you for considering this request. I value our partnership and appreciate your support. Sincerely, [Your Name] [Your Title] [Your Company Name] [Your Email] | [Your Phone Number]
Commentary: This template is ideal when your client has a specific connection to the prospective client. It offers options based on their comfort level while acknowledging the existing relationship.
Tips for Adaptation: Research the specific connection between your client and the prospect to make the ask more targeted. For closer relationships, you can be more direct with the request.
